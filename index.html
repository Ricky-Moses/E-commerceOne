Leafy Greens


Leafy greens are some of the most nutrient-dense vegetables, often rich in vitamins A, C and K, folate, iron, and calcium and are also a great source of antioxidants.

  • Spinach
  • Kale
  • Collard Greens

Root Vegetable


Root vegetables are the underground part of a plant, allowing them to absorb a lot nutrients from the soil. They are usually high in complex carbohydrates and fiber, making them a good source of energy, and provide essential vitamins and mineral such as potassium and vitamin C

Bulb Vegetable


Bulb vegetables grow underground, and due to their strong flavors, they are often used to add depth and aroma to a variety of dishes.

  • Onions
  • Garlic
  • Shallots
  • Leeks

Stem Vegetable


Stem vegetables are plant stalks that are eaten for their crunchy texture and mild favors. They are often in strifes, salad, or as a raw snack. Stem vegetables are low in calories and high in fiber, which can support digestion.

  • Asparagus
  • Celery

Flower Vegetable


Flower vegetables are the blossoms of plants that are harvested for consumption, and often add unique flavors and texture to dishes. Flowers vegetables are generally low in calories and high in fiber, vitamins, and antioxidants.

  • Broccoli
  • Cauliflower

Fruits Vegetables


Fruits vegetables are the fruits of plants that are not sweets, and are typically used in savory dishes. While they're technically fruits, they are classified as vegetables in culinary terms due to their savory flavors profiles

  • Tomatoes
  • Cucumbers

Pod Vegetables


Pod vegetables are edible plants seed pods, often rich in protein, fiber and vitaminC and K. They are widely used in both raw, cooked and dried forms in various dishes

  • Green Beans
  • Peas
  • Okra
  • Snow Peas

Tuber Vegetables


Often seen as part of the 'root vegetable' family, tubers are a plant's underground storage organs, rich in carbohydrates and often used as a staple food in various cultures. Tubers are rich in carbohydrates, particular starch

  • Potatoes

Seed Vegetables


Seed vegetables are plant whose edible parts are its seeds. Seed vegetables are rich in protein, fat and minerals such as iron and magnesium, and are eaten in many ways

  • Corns
  • Legumes

Mushroom


Although mushrooms are often classified as vegetables in culinary terms, fungi are technically a plant category entirely of its own. They are prized for their umami flavor and versatility in various cuisines.

  • Button Mushroom
  • Shiitake mushrooms
  • Portobella mushrooms
